Thought Record

A thought record can be used when you are having negative or maladaptive thoughts. First, you write down your automatic negative thought in the left box(ex: I am worthless). Then you choose which cognitive distortion this thought corresponds with (note: it can be more than one so just pick the one that suits it best. The list of cognitive distortions with definitons of them is at the bottom of this page to help you decide.) Then, you find a rational response and write it down in the right hand box(ex: I am of value to my friends and family.) This cognitive restructuring helps you reframe your thoughts into more positive ones. Give it a try if you need!

Automatic Thought Cognitive Distortion Rational Response
Cognitive Distortion Definition
All-or-nothing thinking Seeing things as either completely good or completely bad, with no in-between.
Overgeneralization Drawing broad conclusions based on a single incident or piece of evidence.
Mental filter Focusing exclusively on the negative aspects of a situation and ignoring the positive.
Disqualifying the positive Rejecting or dismissing positive experiences or events as being insignificant.
Jumping to conclusions Making assumptions or drawing conclusions without enough evidence to support them.
Magnification and minimization Exaggerating the importance of negative events while downplaying the significance of positive events.
Emotional reasoning Believing that your feelings are automatically true, regardless of the evidence.
Should statements Using "should," "must," or "ought" statements to impose rigid rules on yourself or others.
Labeling and mislabeling Assigning a negative label to yourself or others based on a single event or characteristic.
Personalization Believing that everything that happens around you is directly related to you, even when there is no evidence to support this.
